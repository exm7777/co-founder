# The verify checklist

Three traps make false claims look corroborated. Every claim graded `verified` must survive
all three; a claim that fails any one gets graded `stated` and carried as a quote.

## Trap 1: Single-origin corroboration

Ten articles repeating one press release is ONE source wearing ten hats. Before counting two
sources as independent, trace each back: do they cite the same study, the same announcement,
the same original thread? Same origin means one source. Independence requires separately
produced evidence: a different dataset, a different practitioner's own numbers, a direct
observation.

## Trap 2: Vendor echo

A vendor's claim about its own product, repeated by press and influencers, stays a vendor
claim no matter how many outlets carry it. Vendor numbers are graded `stated` unless a party
with no stake reproduces them. The same discipline applies to gurus quoting their own course
material across platforms.

## Trap 3: Survivor selection

"Five founders did X and grew" says nothing without the ones who did X and did not grow.
When the evidence is success stories, ask what the base rate is and whether anyone counted
the failures. If nobody did, the claim supports "some people succeed doing X", never "X
works". Grade accordingly and say so in the dossier.

## The adversarial pass (deep tier)

For each load-bearing claim, run one dedicated attempt to break it. Where the harness runs
parallel agents, use a fresh agent per claim with no sight of the synthesis; otherwise, a
fresh sequential pass per claim. The attacker's brief, verbatim:

> Try to refute this claim. Hunt the disconfirming source, the trap it fails from the
> checklist, the newer data that supersedes it. Default to refuted when uncertain. Report:
> holds / refuted / weakened, with the evidence.

Claims that come back refuted leave the synthesis. Weakened claims get their grade lowered
and their caveat written next to them. The synthesis is rewritten AFTER the pass, never
patched around it.
